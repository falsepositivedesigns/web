About the Technology

Our apparel leverages digital self-defense with adversarial patterns designed to disrupt facial recognition and tracking AI systems.

Here’s how False Positive works, grounded in established research:

  • Adversarial Patterns: Inspired by academic research such as “Adversarial Examples in the Physical World” (Kurakin et al.), our designs create visual noise that can cause machine learning models to misclassify or fail to detect faces.
  • Camouflage & Adversarial Fashion: Similar to projects like Adversarial Fashion and “Robust Physical-World Attacks on Deep Learning Models” (Eykholt et al.), our clothing integrates patterns specifically engineered to interfere with AI perception in varying conditions.
  • Research-Informed Design: We closely follow advances in adversarial attacks in computer vision to refine our patterns, including use of reflective fabrics and high-contrast elements as discussed in “Physical Adversarial Examples for Camera-based Face Recognition Systems”.
  • Ongoing Evolution: As AI surveillance technology evolves, so do our methods. Inspired by the continuous research community efforts — e.g., Adversarial Machine Learning Resources — we aim to stay adaptive and innovative in our designs.
  • Designed For Privacy Advocates: Our apparel empowers developers, security researchers, and activists who want to take a stand against pervasive surveillance capitalism.

Effectiveness may vary based on conditions and system sophistication. False Positive clothing is a tool for digital self-defense and raising awareness rather than a guaranteed shield.

Disclaimer: No adversarial pattern or clothing can guarantee full protection against all AI surveillance systems. Please wear responsibly and stay informed.
